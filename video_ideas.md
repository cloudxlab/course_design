## Category 1 — The Hook
*Philosophy, contrarian ideas, scroll-stoppers*

### 1. "Design Your Own Number System" `[R]` `[AD]`
Cold open — instructor walks into frame, writes on whiteboard: **"Design a number system. You have 10 minutes."**

Cut to confused faces. Then the penny drops — someone counts in base 7. Then base 2. Then: *"That's why computers work the way they do."*

End card: *"This is Day 1 of The Inventor's Journey."*

> **Why it works:** Hooks on confusion, pays off with genuine insight. Feeling: Curiosity → delight.

---

### 2. "Stop Memorising. Start Inventing." `[R]` `[AD]`
Fast cuts: student highlighting a textbook formula → student staring blankly at an exam → graduate struggling to explain their own degree.

Voice-over: *"Formulas are forgettable. What you invent — you own."*

Cut to student building a decision tree from scratch, explaining it confidently in an interview. End card: tagline + IIT Roorkee logo.

> **Why it works:** Directly names the frustration every Indian student feels.

---

### 3. "What Your College Teaches vs. What Industry Wants" `[R]` `[LI]`
Split screen. Left: lecture hall, slide of a sorting algorithm, rote notes. Right: same student at a computer, building the algorithm from scratch, explaining every line.

Text overlay: *"Same topic. Different understanding. Different outcome."*

> **Why it works:** Highly shareable among college students and parents.

---

### 4. "Can AI Replace You?" `[R]` `[AD]`
Open with the scary framing: *"AI can write code. AI can debug. AI can build models."*

Pause. Then: *"AI cannot think for the first time."*

Show a novel, ambiguous problem. AI gives a mediocre answer. A student who built from first principles spots the flaw immediately and fixes it.

Tagline: *"Learn how AI is built — so you can lead it, not follow it."*

> **Why it works:** Directly addresses the most common objection.

---

### 5. "I Built a Neural Network Before I Knew What It Was Called" `[R]` `[LI]`
Student testimonial, casual selfie-style:

*"The instructor gave us a problem. No theory. No slides. Just: make it predict. I wrote 40 lines of code. Then he said: 'You just wrote a neural network.' I genuinely couldn't speak for a minute."*

> **Why it works:** Authentic, unexpected, shareable.

---

### 6. "The GPS Problem" `[R]`
Sandeep Giri to camera:

*"People ask if they still need to learn to code now that AI can do it. Here's my answer: can you drive somewhere you've never been without GPS? Sure. But what happens the moment GPS fails? The person who understands the road survives. The person who only follows arrows is lost."*

End card: **"Don't just use AI. Understand it."**

> **Why it works:** Simple analogy, quotable, builds authority.

## Category 3 — The Curriculum in Action

### 11. "Recursion in 60 Seconds — The Way It Should Be Taught" `[R]`
Instructor holds up a mirror in front of another mirror. *"You see infinite reflections. That's recursion — a function that calls itself until it reaches a base case."* Physical demo → live code on screen.

*"Once you see it this way, you never forget it."*

> **Why it works:** Educational content that delivers genuine insight in under a minute. Shareable by teachers, students, and developers.

---

### 12. "What 8 Months Looks Like" `[R]` `[LI]`
Timeline video — fast visual montage:

- **Month 1:** You design a number system
- **Month 2:** You write your own sorting algorithm
- **Month 3–4:** You build an ML model — from maths to code
- **Month 5–6:** You implement backpropagation in NumPy
- **Month 7:** You build a Transformer. From scratch.
- **Month 8:** You train an RL agent on an environment you designed

End: Portfolio on GitHub. IIT Roorkee certificate. Job offer.

> **Why it works:** Makes the transformation concrete and trackable.

---

### 13. "Before This Course vs. After" `[R]`
Same student, same problem: *"Write a function that finds the shortest path in a graph."*

- **Before:** Googles it, copies code, pastes it, can't explain it.
- **After:** Draws a diagram, explains BFS from first principles, writes it clean.

Interviewer reaction: *"That's exactly the kind of thinking we hire for."*

> **Why it works:** Shows transformation with a concrete, relatable moment.

---

### 14. "The Kaggle Moment" `[R]` `[LI]`

*"I built this from scratch. It's actually working."*

Cut to profile page: public projects, public portfolio.

*"8 months ago I had never written a line of Python."*

> **Why it works:** Kaggle is recognisable to the tech audience. Concrete, verifiable proof of output.

### 15. "Build an AI Agent in a Weekend" `[R]`

## Category 6 — Punchy One-Liners (Scroll-Stoppers)

### 22. "The Interview Test" `[R]` `[AD]` *(15 seconds)*
Text on screen: *"Interviewer: Explain gradient descent."*

- **Student A (MOOC grad):** *"It's an optimisation algorithm that... minimises a loss function using partial derivatives."*
- **Student B (Inventor's Journey):** *"I'll show you — I implemented it from scratch. Here's my code. Here's why it works."*

**"Know the answer. Or own the answer."**

---

### 23. "What Recursion Really Is" `[R]` *(30 seconds)*
Instructor opens a box. Inside is a smaller box. Inside that, a smaller box.

*"What do you do?"* — *"Keep opening."* — *"Until?"* — *"Until there's no box."*

*"That's recursion. Now write it."*

> **Why it works:** Memorable concept demo. Infinite replay value.

---

### 24. "The Invent vs. Memorise Speed Test" `[R]` *(30 seconds)*
Two students given an unfamiliar variant of a binary search problem.

- One memorised the algorithm. Freezes.
- One invented it. Adapts immediately.

*"Memorising gets you through the exam. Inventing gets you through the interview — and the job."*

---

### 25. "Three Things That Happen When You Invent Your Own Algorithm" `[R]` *(45 seconds)*
Fast, punchy list format:

1. You understand **why** it works — not just that it works
2. You remember it forever — because **you made it**
3. You can adapt it when the problem changes — because **it's yours**

Cut to: *"This is how every module works on The Inventor's Journey."*
